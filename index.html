About Me

I'm a software engineer who loves building systems that work reliably at scale. My journey started with a Bachelor's in Computer & Communication Engineering from Manipal Institute of Technology, where I discovered my passion for distributed systems and backend development.

After graduation, I spent three years at E-Tech Innovative Services, where I led the development of E-Krishi Mandi—a cloud-based marketplace that transformed how agricultural commodities are traded in India. Starting from scratch, I architected a microservices-based system that now serves over 100,000 users and processes millions of transactions annually.

I learned that great engineering isn't just about writing clever code—it's about understanding real problems, designing thoughtful solutions, and maintaining systems in production. I got really comfortable with the full software lifecycle: from debugging production issues at 2am to optimizing databases that were slowing down under load to mentoring junior developers on the team.

Currently, I'm completing my Master's in Computer Science at UIC (graduating May 2026), where I'm working on machine learning research—specifically, making neural networks "forget" specific data points efficiently through machine unlearning techniques.

What drives me is building things that actually work. Not just proof-of-concepts or demos, but systems that handle real traffic, fail gracefully, recover automatically, and scale when needed.
